Case Study #1

Website Design

TLDR:

Problem Statement: Angelica is a recruiter who needs to efficiently see my skills because she has 500 applicants to review.

Goal Statement: Help recruiters who are rushed understand my skills and approach.

My design objective with the website is to concisely communicate my technique in a pleasing format. I am going for an elegant simplicity . This will cater to the time constraint of myself (to develop within 24 hrs) and the end-user (busy recruiter). I chose a straightforward layout combined with minimalistic design elements to convey this. I then use Google's Robo Mono font and black/white color schemes as an homage to my technical background. Further more, this allows me to then use color in impactful ways to summarize information.

Skills Used:

Adobe XD | Wireframing | Rapid Protoyping | CSS/HTML

Design Approach

My goal for this project is to design and develop the website within 24 hours. One thing I have learned is to focus on doing the simple things well before focusing on anything else. I think this strategy will help to focus my efforts as well as align with the end-user (busy recruiter). If the end-user is short on time, putting myself in that position will help me design for them.

I know I wanted something straighforward and simple. I went with a classic portfolio layout so that it would immediately be recognizable and comfortable to a user. This includes a headshot on the left with my name prominently displayed, my title, and a quick summary. I then added a small menu bar to the top right. Simple enough. Users should be able to enter here and immdeiately feel they know where they are and how to get around.
I then wanted my projects listed below in two columns. In order to coax the user down the page, I decided to have the top of the project photos visible above the fold. This should convey scrollability and draw the user's attention downward.
Finally, the user should land at the bottom in a small footer section. This area has a call to action and links to connect.

While implementing, I made some design changes. I added a box around my name which links to my linkedin (about me once that page is finished). This communicates a clear way for users to get info about me if they are interested. The cursor typing animation and color swap on-hover add just enough interactivity for the user to be entertained.

Overall, I think the project was a success. Given more time I would like to focus on accesibility and mobile layout.
